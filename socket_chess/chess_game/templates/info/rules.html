Chess Rules Overview

There are 16 pieces each player has. Below are the pieces and their corresponding moves.

  • 8 Pawns: Moves forward one square. It can also move forward 2 squares at its very first move. This piece captures one square diagonally. There is also a special move called "En Passant".
  • 2 Rooks: Moves horizontally or vertically any number of squares, provided it is not blocked by any other piece. It can also perform a combined move with the King, known as "castling".
  • 2 Knights: Moves one square horizontally/vertically and one square diagonally in order. It can jump over other pieces and captures an opposing piece if it lands on it.
  • 2 Bishops: Moves diagonally any number of squares, provided it is not blocked by any other piece.
  • 1 Queen: Moves horizontally, vertically, or diagonally any number of squares. One of the strongest pieces in the arsenal of a chess player.
  • 1 King: Moves horizontally, vertically, or diagonally exactly one square. It is the most important piece, weakest in the early game, but becomes stronger as the game progresses into the late game.
